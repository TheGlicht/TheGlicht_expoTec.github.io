INTRODUCTION

Why use black box testing techniques?

  • They help to determine negative test cases in a more efficient way.
  • They make the test cases with the highest probability of having defects more visible.
  • Saves redundant test design and execution.
  • Help to measure the percentage of test coverage.


Software testing plays a crucial role in the application development process, ensuring that programs work reliably and meet the requirements set. Within testing strategies, two essential approaches are the "Equivalence Partitioning Technique" and "Boundary Values". These techniques allow test engineers to identify critical scenarios and maximize test coverage while minimizing the effort required.
